1. Assessment Philosophy: Behavioral Evidence Over Self-Report
Traditional psychometric surveys ask students what they think they are like. Mini-games and simulations capture what they actually do under controlled conditions. This is a fundamental shift: behavior does not lie the way self-report does. A student cannot easily fake their reaction time, error rate under pressure, or persistence through a frustrating puzzle series.
Evidence Tier
What It Measures
Examples
Tier 1 — Behavioral (Strongest)
Measured by actual performance: speed, accuracy, choices, error patterns
Reaction time, attention to detail, negotiation simulation, pattern recognition, fine motor trace
Tier 2 — Simulated Choice (Strong)
Student chooses between realistic scenarios — indirect behavioral signal
Career swipe, workplace environment explorer, life design game, conversation fork
Tier 3 — Stimulus-Based Survey (Acceptable)
Self-report on concrete, observable stimuli — only where Tier 1/2 impossible
Physical strength self-report, stamina (cannot be digitally tested)

3. Full Module Specifications
GROUP A — Original Modules M1–M8  |  Session 1 (M1–M4) + Session 2 (M5–M8)
M1
Interest Explorer
Session 1  |  ~12 min
66 behavioral events  |  Tier 1 (Career Swipe — behavioral) + Tier 2 (Workplace Tour — simulated choice)
Category
Interests & RIASEC
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Interests: Realistic (R), Investigative (I), Artistic (A), Social (S), Enterprising (E), Conventional (C) — all 6 codes, 10+ data points each
How It Works
Students encounter 60 activity cards — images or short animated clips showing real work activities. Swipe right (Like), left (Dislike), up (Neutral). Reaction time secretly recorded: fast likes are stronger signals than slow deliberated ones. After the swipe game, a Workplace Tour: walkthrough of 6 simulated environments (workshop, lab, studio, office, clinic, school) where time-on-screen and engagement clicks per environment are logged.
Games & Tasks
Career Swipe (60 cards: 10 per RIASEC code — activities, images, clips) | Workplace Tour (6 environments: dwell time + click engagement logged per environment)
Scoring
Each swipe: +2 (fast like <600ms), +1 (slow like), 0 (neutral/skip), -1 (dislike). Workplace Tour dwell time normalised 0–10 per environment. Final RIASEC score: weighted sum per code, scaled 0–100. IRT calibration applied to swipe items after pilot data collected.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (swipe card mechanic with animated transitions and reaction time logging via Date.now())
M2
Work Styles Arena
Session 1  |  ~18 min
~151 behavioral events across 8 games  |  Tier 1 — Behavioral (all 8 games are performance-based; no self-report)
Category
Personality / Work Styles
O*NET Dimensions
All 21 O*NET Work Styles: Achievement Orientation, Adaptability, Attention to Detail, Cautiousness, Cooperation, Dependability, Empathy, Humility, Initiative, Innovation, Integrity, Intellectual Curiosity, Leadership Orientation, Optimism, Perseverance, Self-Confidence, Self-Control, Sincerity, Social Orientation, Stress Tolerance, Tolerance for Ambiguity
How It Works
Eight mini-games — each game mechanic IS the measurement. Student never answers "how do you rate yourself on X?" — their behavior in the game generates the score. Each game targets 2–4 Work Style dimensions simultaneously.
Games & Tasks
Error Hunt (Attention to Detail, Dependability, Cautiousness) — proofread 8 documents with embedded errors | Pressure Lab (Stress Tolerance, Self-Control, Cautiousness) — escalating multi-task puzzle with interruptions | Idea Storm (Innovation, Originality, Intellectual Curiosity, Tolerance for Ambiguity) — alternative uses + unfinished sketches | Persistence Climb (Achievement Orientation, Perseverance, Self-Confidence) — increasingly hard puzzles with Try Again/Hint/Skip choices | Social Lens (Empathy, Social Orientation, Cooperation, Humility, Sincerity) — scenario choices + micro-expression recognition + fault attribution | First Mover (Initiative, Leadership Orientation, Self-Confidence) — ambiguous inbox decisions + group leadership scenarios | Curveball (Adaptability, Tolerance for Ambiguity, Self-Control) — sorting game with silent rule changes | Integrity Check (Integrity, Dependability, Sincerity) — ethical scenarios + consistency trap
Scoring
Each game generates raw behavioral scores (accuracy, speed, error rate, choice patterns). IRT or z-score normalisation converts raw counts to 0–100 dimension scores anchored to O*NET 1–5 importance scale.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (8 separate scene modules, shared score bus, single entry point)
M3
Values & Life Design
Session 1  |  ~8 min
~34 decision events + 6 survey items  |  Tier 2 — Simulated Choice (forced-choice scenarios) + minimal Tier 3 (6 stimulus-anchored survey items)
Category
Work Values
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Work Values: Achievement, Independence, Recognition, Relationships, Support, Working Conditions — all 6 dimensions, 5–7 data points each
How It Works
Job offer forced-choice simulation: student picks which of two job offers to accept. Each pair is designed to force a trade-off between two work values. 5 pairs per value = 15 forced choices. Then a Schedule Builder game: student constructs an ideal work week from blocks (deep work, meetings, social, solo, creative, admin). Block allocation pattern reveals values under realistic constraint.
Games & Tasks
Job Offer Cards (15 forced-choice pairs — each pair tests one value trade-off) | Schedule Builder (drag-and-drop week planner, 7 block types, 40 hours to allocate) | 6 stimulus-anchored survey items (e.g., "Rate how important job security is" shown alongside a workplace image)
Scoring
Forced choice: each accepted offer scores +1 for its dominant value. Schedule Builder: time allocation per block type weighted and mapped to value dimensions. Survey items: 1–5 Likert, used to cross-validate choice behavior.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (card choice UI with flip animation + drag-and-drop scheduler with time counter)
M4
Environment Simulator
Session 1  |  ~10 min
45 stimuli + rating responses  |  Tier 2 — Simulated Choice (students rate comfort with realistic workplace images/clips)
Category
Work Context
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Work Context: all 55 dimensions across Physical Environment, Workplace Environment, Social Context, Structural Job Characteristics (physical demands, hazard exposure, communication mode, interpersonal context, job demands)
How It Works
Workplace scene explorer: 45 environment stimuli shown as images or short animated clips representing real workplace conditions. Student rates comfort and preference on a simple 3-point scale (Comfortable / Neutral / Uncomfortable). Covers: outdoor vs. indoor, noise levels, hazard presence, social density, physical demand, technology level, autonomy vs. supervision.
Games & Tasks
45 scene stimuli across Work Context sub-categories | Rating interface (3-point comfort scale + optional open "Why?" field for qualitative signal)
Scoring
Each stimulus mapped to 1–3 Work Context O*NET dimensions. Comfort ratings averaged per dimension. Extreme discomfort flags (3+ uncomfortable in a cluster) are surfaced in the career report as context constraints.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (image carousel with rating overlay; stimuli pre-loaded as image assets)
M5
Cognitive Lab (Extended)
Session 2  |  ~20 min
100–130 trials (adaptive)  |  Tier 1 — Behavioral ability tests with IRT adaptive difficulty
Category
Cognitive Abilities
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Abilities: Oral Comprehension, Written Comprehension, Written Expression, Fluency of Ideas, Originality, Memorisation, Problem Sensitivity, Mathematical Reasoning, Number Facility, Deductive Reasoning, Inductive Reasoning, Information Ordering, plus Perceptual Speed, Category Flexibility (via M13)
How It Works
13 distinct cognitive ability tasks. IRT engine selects item difficulty based on prior responses. Each task is a separate Phaser.js scene. Tasks are ordered from lowest to highest cognitive load. Reaction time, accuracy, and error pattern all contribute to scoring.
Games & Tasks
Pattern Completion (Inductive Reasoning — find the rule in a matrix of shapes) | Sentence Completion (Oral/Written Comprehension — choose the correct word) | Number Series (Mathematical Reasoning — find next number in sequence) | Rotation Match (Spatial Orientation — which rotated shape matches?) | Reading Comprehension (Written Comprehension — 150-word passage + 4 inference questions) | Number Estimation (Number Facility — rapid approximate arithmetic) | Symbol Substitution (Processing Speed — replace symbols per key as fast as possible) | Error Detection (Perceptual Speed — spot the changed element in a 5×5 grid) | Sentence Unscramble (Deductive Reasoning — reconstruct logical sentence from jumbled words) | Category Sort (Inductive Reasoning — group items by discovered rule) | Figure Series (Fluid Intelligence — predict next figure in animated sequence) | Arithmetic Story Problems (Mathematical Reasoning — word problems with time pressure) | Word Definition (Verbal Ability — choose best definition from 4 options)
Scoring
IRT-based scoring: item difficulty (b parameter) calibrated in pilot. Student ability (theta) estimated via maximum likelihood after each response. Final score per dimension expressed as theta with confidence interval. Percentile ranking computed vs. peer group.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (13 task scenes with shared IRT engine in JavaScript; adaptive item bank of 20+ items per task generated by Claude API)
M6
Skills Arena
Session 2  |  ~18 min
80–90 scenario-based tasks  |  Tier 1 — Performance-based scenario tests (student demonstrates skill, not reports it)
Category
O*NET Skills
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Skills (12 of 35): Reading Comprehension, Active Listening, Writing, Speaking, Mathematics, Critical Thinking, Active Learning, Social Perceptiveness, Coordination, Persuasion, Judgment and Decision Making, Complex Problem Solving. New T1–T7 modules cover remaining 23 technical/systems/resource management skills.
How It Works
Six scenario-based performance tests. Each test places the student in a realistic task where the skill being measured is required to succeed. Scores emerge from what students do, not what they say about themselves.
Games & Tasks
Comprehension Sprint (Reading Comprehension, Active Learning) — read 200-word passage, answer 4 timed inference Qs | Rapid Report (Writing, Active Learning) — given 5 bullet facts, write a 3-sentence summary scored by Claude API for clarity and completeness | Listen & Act (Active Listening) — audio clip plays once, student answers 5 comprehension Qs | Number Challenge (Mathematics) — 10 word problems under time pressure | Logic Ladder (Critical Thinking, Judgment and Decision Making) — 5 argument/decision puzzles | Team Dilemma (Social Perceptiveness, Coordination, Persuasion, Complex Problem Solving) — branching team scenario with 8 decision points
Scoring
Each task scored on accuracy and time. Writing tasks: Claude API returns structured score (clarity 0–5, completeness 0–5, accuracy 0–5). Listening tasks: audio played once only (no replay) — penalise misses more than errors. Team Dilemma: prosocial choices vs. self-serving, quality of reasoning in decision points.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (6 scene modules) + Web Audio API (Listen & Act) + Claude API (Rapid Report writing scoring)
M7
Expert Challenge
Session 3  |  ~15 min
Adaptive: 8–12 items per active domain  |  Tier 1 — IRT adaptive MCQ with AI-generated items (prevents cheating/memorisation)
Category
Knowledge (All 33 Domains)
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Knowledge: Administration and Management, Clerical/Administrative, Economics and Accounting, Sales and Marketing, Customer and Personal Service, Personnel and Human Resources, Computers and Electronics, Engineering and Technology, Design, Building and Construction, Mechanical, Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Psychology, Sociology and Anthropology, Geography, Medicine and Dentistry, Therapy and Counseling, Education and Training, English Language, Foreign Language, Fine Arts, History and Archeology, Philosophy and Theology, Public Safety and Security, Law and Government, Telecommunications, Communications and Media, Transportation, Food Production, Production and Processing
How It Works
IRT-adaptive MCQ system. Starts with breadth screening (2–3 items per domain from the student's top 6 shortlisted domains based on Session 1–2 results). Strong performance in a domain triggers depth probing (8–12 items). Items generated by Claude API for each session — no item bank to memorise. Student experiences it as a fast-paced knowledge trivia game with subject-specific aesthetics.
Games & Tasks
Breadth screening round (2–3 items per shortlisted domain) | Depth round (8–12 adaptive items in confirmed domains) | Items generated by Claude API per session (unique every time)
Scoring
IRT theta estimation after each item. Domains above theta threshold receive depth probing. Final knowledge profile: theta score per active domain + confidence interval. Compared against O*NET knowledge importance ratings for matched occupations.
Tech Stack
Claude API (item generation with domain + difficulty parameters) + Phaser.js (MCQ interface with subject-themed aesthetics per domain) + IRT engine (JavaScript)
M8
Physical & Sensory Lab
Session 2  |  ~8 min
40 behavioral trials + 4 survey items  |  Tier 1 (screen-measurable) + Tier 3 (4 survey items for non-digital dimensions)
Category
Physical & Sensory Abilities
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Physical Abilities: Near Vision, Far Vision proxy, Colour Discrimination, Depth Perception, Reaction Time, Wrist-Finger Speed, Speed of Limb Movement, Arm-Hand Steadiness. Self-report (SR): Explosive Strength, Static Strength, Trunk Strength, Stamina
How It Works
Screen-measurable sensory and motor tests combined with a minimal self-report section (4 items) for dimensions genuinely impossible to test on screen (strength, stamina). The self-report items use concrete observable stimuli (job images) rather than abstract trait questions.
Games & Tasks
Colour Hue Discrimination (Farnsworth-Munsell inspired sort game — arrange colour chips in gradient order, scored on error count) | Visual Target Detection (progressively smaller targets in noise — Near Vision proxy) | Reaction Time Tap (tap when target appears, 20 trials, mean + variance) | Steady Trace (arm-hand steadiness — trace path with mouse, deviation measured) | Sound Pattern Match (Web Audio — reproduce a 4-note rhythm pattern by tapping) | Physical Ability Survey (4 items: strength self-assessment, stamina estimate, physical job comfort — shown with job-context images)
Scoring
Colour sort: number of out-of-order chips (lower = better). Reaction time: mean RT + standard deviation (consistency signal). Target detection: threshold size at 75% accuracy. Trace: average pixel deviation from path. Sound match: pattern accuracy score. Survey items: 1–5 Likert, flagged as Tier 3 in report.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (all game modules) + Web Audio API (sound pattern match) + touch/mouse event tracking
GROUP B — Cognitive Extension M9–M22  |  Session 2
M9
Dual N-Back Lite
Session 2  |  ~4 min
~40 adaptive trials  |  Tier 1 — Behavioral (dual-task working memory paradigm)
Category
Working Memory / Cognitive Extension
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Abilities: Working Memory Capacity; also signals: Auditory Attention, Selective Attention, Divided Attention
How It Works
Two simultaneous streams: a letter spoken aloud via Web Audio, and a grid position lit up on screen. Student taps a button when the current letter matches what appeared N steps back, and another button when the grid position matches. N starts at 1 and increases adaptively based on accuracy. Classic dual N-back paradigm — gold standard for working memory measurement.
Games & Tasks
Dual N-Back task: letter stream (audio) + position stream (visual) | N-level adapts from 1-back to 3-back based on accuracy (80%+ triggers increase) | 40 trials per session
Scoring
Hit rate and false alarm rate per N-level. d' (signal detection theory) calculated per stream. Working memory capacity score = maximum N-level sustained above 75% accuracy for >10 trials.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (visual grid) + Web Audio API (letter audio playback) + adaptive N-level controller
M10
Split Track
Session 2  |  ~4 min
30 dual-task trials  |  Tier 1 — Behavioral (dual-task performance cost paradigm)
Category
Time Sharing / Divided Attention
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Abilities: Time Sharing; O*NET Skills: Time Management (behavioral proxy)
How It Works
Two simultaneous tasks displayed side-by-side. Left panel: falling objects sorted by colour (tap left/right bin). Right panel: numbers appearing — tap if odd, ignore if even. Scoring uses dual-task cost: performance on each task measured alone first (single-task baseline), then together. The cost = baseline minus dual-task performance.
Games & Tasks
Single-task baseline (2 min each task alone) | Dual-task trials (both tasks simultaneously, 30 trials) | Score = dual-task cost index
Scoring
Dual-task cost calculated separately for each subtask. Composite Time Sharing score = 1 - (average cost). Higher score = lower performance degradation under divided attention. Normative comparison to peer group.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (two independent game groups in single scene, running simultaneously)
M11
Go/No-Go
Session 2  |  ~3 min
60 trials (40 Go, 20 No-Go)  |  Tier 1 — Behavioral (classic inhibitory control paradigm)
Category
Impulse Control / Sustained Attention
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Abilities: Selective Attention; O*NET Work Styles: Self-Control, Cautiousness
How It Works
Shapes appear rapidly on screen. Student taps for circles (Go — 40 trials) and must NOT tap for red circles (No-Go — 20 trials, interspersed). Speed and accuracy both scored. Later rounds introduce distractors (animated borders, similar-looking shapes). False alarms (tapping No-Go) penalised more heavily than misses.
Games & Tasks
Go trials (40: tap circle as fast as possible) | No-Go trials (20: inhibit response to red circle) | Distractor phase (rounds 2–3: visual noise added around stimuli)
Scoring
False alarm rate (key inhibitory control metric), miss rate, mean RT on Go trials, RT variability (sustained attention). Composite Impulse Control score = weighted combination. Normative percentile vs. peer group.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (fast animation loop, response time logging to millisecond, keyboard + touch input)
M12
Colour-Word Stroop
Session 2  |  ~4 min
50 trials across 4 conditions  |  Tier 1 — Behavioral (classic Stroop paradigm)
Category
Cognitive Flexibility / Processing Speed
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Abilities: Selective Attention, Perceptual Speed; O*NET Work Styles: Self-Control, Adaptability
How It Works
The word "RED" appears in blue ink. Student must respond with the INK COLOUR, not the word meaning. Four conditions in sequence: (1) colour patches only — baseline, (2) colour words, congruent — easy, (3) colour words, incongruent — Stroop interference, (4) mixed random — cognitive flexibility demand. Stroop interference score = RT difference between congruent and incongruent conditions.
Games & Tasks
Condition 1: colour patches (8 trials, baseline RT) | Condition 2: congruent words (12 trials) | Condition 3: incongruent words (18 trials, interference measured) | Condition 4: mixed (12 trials, flexibility measured)
Scoring
Stroop interference index = mean RT(incongruent) - mean RT(congruent). Flexibility index = performance recovery in condition 4. Both normalised to peer group. Lower interference = better cognitive control.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (text rendering with programmable font colour, RT logging, keyboard/button response)
M13
Rule Shift
Session 2  |  ~5 min
~40 trials with 2 rule shifts  |  Tier 1 — Behavioral (Wisconsin Card Sorting Test inspired)
Category
Category Flexibility / Adaptability
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Abilities: Category Flexibility; O*NET Work Styles: Adaptability, Tolerance for Ambiguity
How It Works
Cards appear with three attributes: colour, shape, number. Student sorts each card by clicking a category. Initially one sorting rule applies (e.g., sort by colour). After 15 correct sorts, the rule silently changes (now sort by shape) — no announcement. Student must detect the change from feedback alone and shift strategy. A second rule change occurs later.
Games & Tasks
Phase 1: Sort by colour (rule given at start, 15 trials) | Phase 2: Rule silently shifts to shape (student detects via error feedback, ~12 trials) | Phase 3: Rule silently shifts to number (student detects, ~13 trials)
Scoring
Perseverative errors (continuing to sort by old rule after shift detected) — key rigidity measure. Trials to detect each shift (lower = more flexible). Total correct sorts. Composite Category Flexibility score.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (card display with animated sort feedback; rule controller never shown to student)
M14
Name-Face-Role
Session 2  |  ~5 min
16 recall items (8 name + 8 role)  |  Tier 1 — Behavioral (episodic associative memory task)
Category
Associative Memory
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Abilities: Memorisation, Associative Memory; relevant for: sales, HR, management, teaching, healthcare careers
How It Works
8 "people" shown for 5 seconds each — name, AI-generated face image, and job title. Study phase (no pressure, just viewing). Then test phase: face shown alone → student types name; name shown alone → student selects role from 4 options. Order randomised. Designed to simulate real-world professional memory demands.
Games & Tasks
Study phase (8 people × 5 seconds each) | Name recall test (8 trials: face → type name) | Role recall test (8 trials: name → select role from 4 options)
Scoring
Name recall: exact match = 2 pts, close match (1 letter off) = 1 pt. Role recall: correct = 2 pts, adjacent career = 1 pt. Total out of 32. Converted to 0–100 Associative Memory score.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (face card display, text input with fuzzy matching, 4-choice role selector)
M15
Story Sequence
Session 2  |  ~4 min
3 sequences × 6 steps = 18 ordering decisions  |  Tier 1 — Behavioral (sequence reconstruction task)
Category
Information Ordering
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Abilities: Information Ordering; O*NET Skills: Active Learning (applying sequential logic)
How It Works
Three real-world processes are shown in jumbled order (6 steps each). Student drags steps into correct sequence. Three domain-neutral scenarios: (1) scientific experiment setup, (2) patient triage and intake, (3) project launch sequence. Scored on number of correct adjacencies (partial credit), not just binary correct/incorrect.
Games & Tasks
Scenario 1: Scientific experiment (6 steps — domain neutral) | Scenario 2: Patient/client intake (6 steps) | Scenario 3: Project launch (6 steps) | Drag-and-drop reordering for each
Scoring
Adjacency score per sequence: each correctly adjacent pair = 1 point (max 5 per sequence). Total: 0–15 adjacency points, converted to 0–100 Information Ordering score.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (drag-and-drop list reordering with snap-to-position)
M16
Micro-Read
Session 2  |  ~5 min
20 face stimuli  |  Tier 1 — Behavioral (rapid emotional face recognition paradigm)
Category
Social Perceptiveness / Emotion Recognition
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Skills: Social Perceptiveness; O*NET Work Styles: Empathy, Social Orientation; relevant for healthcare, counselling, HR, sales, teaching
How It Works
A face appears for 200–500ms (exposure time decreases as student improves), then student selects the displayed emotion from 6 options. Includes basic emotions (happy, sad, angry, fearful, disgusted, surprised) and complex/ambiguous ones (contempt, pride, discomfort, embarrassment). Confidence calibration: student also rates confidence (1–3); overconfident wrong answers penalised.
Games & Tasks
20 face stimuli (12 basic emotion, 8 complex/ambiguous) | Exposure time: 500ms rounds 1–10, 300ms rounds 11–16, 200ms rounds 17–20 | Post-response confidence rating (1–3)
Scoring
Accuracy per emotion category. Ambiguous emotion accuracy weighted higher. Confidence calibration score (Brier score): rewards accurate confidence, penalises overconfident errors. Composite Social Perceptiveness score.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (rapid image display with fade + 6-button response overlay; pre-rendered face stimulus set)
M17
Conversation Fork
Session 2  |  ~6 min
4 scenarios × 3 decision points = 12 choices  |  Tier 2 — Simulated Choice (realistic consequence branching)
Category
Social Judgment / Interpersonal Skill
O*NET Dimensions
O*NET Skills: Social Perceptiveness, Service Orientation, Coordination; O*NET Work Styles: Cooperation, Empathy, Self-Control
How It Works
Text-based branching scenarios in realistic social/workplace situations. An ambiguous situation unfolds. Three decision points per scenario, each with 3 response options. After each choice, the consequence plays out visibly (not hidden). Student can see what happened from their choice before the next decision. Scenarios: (1) team conflict, (2) difficult customer, (3) peer underperformance, (4) ethical grey area.
Games & Tasks
4 scenarios, each with 3 decision points | Each decision: 3 response options (vary in empathy, assertiveness, professionalism) | Consequence shown after each choice before proceeding
Scoring
Prosocial choice rate (proportion of choices prioritising others' wellbeing). Escalation avoidance score (choosing de-escalation vs. confrontation). Consistency across scenarios. Composite Social Judgment score.
Tech Stack
Phaser.js (text adventure engine with branching state machine) or HTML with JavaScript state controller
M18
Alternative Uses (AI-Scored)
Session 2  |  ~5 min
